Volcanic Facts

Volcanoes are openings in Earth's crust where molten rock, gases, and ash escape from deep within the planet. They have shaped our world for billions of years.

🔥 1,500+ Active

There are approximately 1,500 potentially active volcanoes worldwide, with around 50-70 erupting each year.

🌡️ Extreme Heat

Lava temperatures range from 700°C to 1,200°C — hot enough to melt most metals instantly.

🌊 Ocean Floor

About 80% of volcanic eruptions occur beneath the ocean, along mid-ocean ridges.

🏔️ Tallest Peak

Mauna Kea in Hawaii is the world's tallest volcano at 10,203m from its oceanic base to summit.

Types of Volcanoes

Volcanoes come in different shapes and sizes, each with unique characteristics and eruption styles.

🗻

Stratovolcano

Steep, cone-shaped peaks built from alternating lava and ash layers. Examples: Mount Fuji, Mount St. Helens.

🛡️

Shield Volcano

Broad, gently sloping domes formed by fluid lava flows. Examples: Mauna Loa, Kilauea.

⛰️

Cinder Cone

Small, steep-sided cones built from volcanic debris. The simplest volcano type.

🕳️

Caldera

Large depressions formed when a volcano collapses after a massive eruption. Example: Yellowstone.

How Eruptions Happen

Volcanic eruptions occur when pressure from molten rock and gases builds up beneath Earth's surface until it finds a way to escape.

1. Magma Formation

Deep beneath the surface (50-200km), extreme heat melts rock into magma. This molten material is less dense than surrounding rock.

2. Rising Pressure

Magma rises through cracks in the crust, collecting in chambers. Dissolved gases expand as pressure decreases.

3. Pre-Eruption Signs

Earthquakes, ground swelling, gas emissions, and temperature changes signal an impending eruption.

4. Eruption

When pressure exceeds the strength of overlying rock, magma bursts through as lava, ash, and volcanic gases.

5. Aftermath

Lava cools and solidifies, adding new rock. Ash settles over vast areas, eventually enriching soil with minerals.

Famous Volcanoes

Throughout history, certain volcanoes have captured human imagination through their beauty, power, or devastating eruptions.

🗾 Mount Fuji, Japan

Japan's iconic stratovolcano and highest peak at 3,776m. Sacred in Japanese culture, last erupted in 1707.

🇮🇹 Mount Vesuvius, Italy

Famous for destroying Pompeii in 79 AD. One of the world's most dangerous volcanoes with 3 million people nearby.

🇺🇸 Yellowstone, USA

A supervolcano with a caldera 72km long. Its last super-eruption 640,000 years ago covered half of North America in ash.

🇮🇸 Eyjafjallajökull, Iceland

Its 2010 eruption disrupted air travel across Europe for weeks, stranding millions of passengers worldwide.
